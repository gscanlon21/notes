# Terminology

**Assay**
: An assay is an investigative (analytic) procedure for qualitatively assessing or quantitatively measuring the presence, amount, or functional activity of a target entity. 
: The measured entity is often called the analyte, the measurand, or the target of the assay.

**Transfection**
: The process of deliberately introducing naked or purified nucleic acids into eukaryotic cells.

**Transformation**
: Used to describe non-viral DNA transfer in bacteria and non-animal eukaryotic cells, including plant cells. 
: In animal cells, transfection is the preferred term, as the term "transformation" is also used to refer to a cell's progression to a cancerous state (carcinogenesis). 

**Transduction**
: Used to describe virus-mediated gene transfer into prokaryotic cells.
