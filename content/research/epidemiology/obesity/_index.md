**Caenorhabditis elegans as a model for obesity research**
Zheng et al. 2011 
_[Review Article](/doi/10.1038/ijo.2011.93.pdf)_ 
: "Caenorhabditis elegans (C. elegans) is a small nematode that conserves 65% of the genes associated with human disease, has a 21-day lifespan, reproductive cycles of 3 days, large brood sizes, lives in an agar dish and does not require committee approvals for experimentation."
: "Despite the continued need of rodent experiments, these attributes make studies of C. elegans an excellent transition from in vitro methods into higher animal models, then into humans in a more efficient and much less expensive manner."
