    My Passion

    I'm interested in how genetics and nutrients interact. So how different gene variants impact nutrient absorption, or how things like the RDA or AI change depending on your genetic variants.

    Describe your academic and career goals and how an NSF opportunity will help you achieve those goals.

    Hi. It's taken me a while to discover myself. Now, after 26 years I can confidently say that two principles I live by are to observe and to share. Take a bird for example, an owl, and look at it's pattern of feathers. You're the only being in existence that sees what you see, hears the slight strokes of the feathers. Without you, that part of existence wouldn't be materialized. I think that's a very powerful thing.

    I would like to get to the place in life where I both have the ability and capability to share the things I've learned in life, but I'm not there yet. Right now I'm still learning. I have had great role models and doctors that have showed my the direction I want to take in life. Now I'm pursuing that by going back to school. Learning is interesting to me because, with each person having their own unique experience, you are able to see a tiny fragment of those unique experiences that other people share. I want to learn, it fulfills a part of my life and makes me happy.

    I'd like to expand this to my whole life. Career-wise, I'd like to break into a healthcare related field. I come from a software background, and I like working with it, but I'd like to use my skills to share a bit of scientific knowledge with others. This opportunity would be great for helping make that transition. On a personal level, I'm inspired by the citizen science website: https://microbiomeprescription.com/. In a similar vein, I want to incorporate academic research into one of my personal projects to help people like me.
