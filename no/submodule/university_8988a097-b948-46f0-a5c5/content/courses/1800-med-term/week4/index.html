    Cardiovascular System

    Structures of the Cardiovascular System

    Heart card/o, cardi/o
    Receives blood from the veins and pumps blood into the arteries
    Blood Vessels angi/o, vas/o
    Transport blood to and from all areas of the body
    Arteries arteri/o
    Transport blood away from the heart to all parts of the body
    Capillaries capill/o
    Permit the exchange of nutrients and waste products between the blood and the cells
    Veins phleb/o, ven/o
    Return blood from all body parts to the heart
    Blood hem/o, hemat/o
    Brings oxygen and nutrients to the cells and carries away waste

    Blood Vessels

    Arteries
    Vessels which carry blood away from the heart
    Capillaries
    The smallest type of blood vessel which connects an arteriole (small artery) to a venule (small vein) to form a network of blood vessels in almost all parts of the body; involved in the exchange of fluids and gases between tissues and the blood
    Veins
    Low-pressure vessels which collect blood to return to the heart

    Blood

    Plasma
    Liquid portion of blood containing water, proteins, and small solutes
    Formed Elements
    Contain erythrocytes (red blood cells), leukocytes (white blood cells) and platelets; the platelets and leukocytes are referred to as the buffy coat
    Erythrocytes
    Contain hemoglobin and transport oxygen
    Leukocytes
    Are involved in the immune system and defense against pathogens
    Platelets
    Also known as thrombocytes
    Formed elements which plate a role in blood clotting

    Specialties

    Cardiologist
    Physician who specializes in diagnosing and treating abnormalities, diseases and disorders of the heart
    Hematologist
    Physician who specializes in diagnosing and treating disorders of the blood and blood forming tissues
    Vascular surgeon
    Physician who specializes in the diagnosis, management and surgical treatment of disorders of the blood vessels
    Telemetry nurse
    Specializes in the use of technology to track vital signs such as blood pressure, heart rhythms and oxygen levels of patients

    Pathologies

    Congenital heart defect
    Structural abnormalities caused by the failure of the heart to develop normally before birth and are the most common type of birth defect
    Coronary artery disease
    Also known as coronary heart disease (CHD) and ischemic heart disease, is atherosclerosis of the coronary arteries that reduces the blood supply to the heart muscle; this can lead to a myocardial infarction (heart attack)
    Atherosclerosis
    Is hardening and narrowing of the arteries caused by a buildup of cholesterol plaque on the interior walls of the arteries (ather/o means plaque or fatty substance, and -sclerosis means abnormal hardening)
    Ischemia
    Condition in which there is an insufficient supply of oxygen in the tissues due to restricted blood flow to a part of the body (isch means to hold back, and -emia means blood)
    Angina
    Also known as angina pectoris, is a condition in which severe episodes of chest pain occur due to an inadequate blood flow to the myocardium
    Myocardial infarction
    Commonly known as a heart attack, is the occlusion (blockage) of one or more coronary arteries caused by plaque buildup
    Heart failure
    Chronic condition in which the heart is unable to pump out all of the blood that it receives. The decreased pumping action causes congestion (fluid build up)
    Carditis
    Inflammation of the heart (card means heart, and -itis means inflammation)
    Cardiomyopathy
    Term used to describe all diseases of the heart muscle (cardi/o means heart, my/o means muscle, and -pathy means disease)
    Arrythmia
    The loss of the normal rhythm of the heartbeat
    Fibrillation
    A potentially life-threatening fast and irregular heartbeat, compared to the fast but regular rhythm of tachycardia
    Aneurysm
    A localized weak spot or balloon-like enlargement of the wall of an artery. The rupture of an aneurysm can be fatal because of the rapid loss of blood
    Varicose vein
    Abnormally swollen veins that usually occur in the superficial veins of the legs. This condition occurs when the valves in these veins do not function properly, so blood pools in the veins, causing them to enlarge
    Thrombosis
    The abnormal condition of having a thrombus (thromb means clot, and -osis means abnormal condition or disease)
    Anemia
    A lower-than-normal number of erythrocytes (red blood cells) in the blood (an- means without or less than, and -emia means blood condition) causing fatigue and shortness of breath

    Lymphatic and Immune System

    Structures of the Lymphatic and Immune System

    The lymphatic system includes lymphatic vessels, tissues such as the tonsils and organs such as the spleen and thymus.

    Vessels
    Lymphatic vessels are unique blind ended vessels, sealed on one end, which picks up excess interstitial fluid in the surrounding area and is then termed lymph. Lymph flows through lymphatic vessels on its way back to the heart to re-enter the cardiovascular system. On its journey, it will pass through various lymph nodes to be checked for any unwanted pathogens.
    Tissue
    Lymphoid tissue, also referred to as Mucosa-associated lymphatic tissue (MALT) is tissue in which various immune cells such as lymphocytes are housed. Examples of MALT include tonsils, peyers patches in the intestines, and the appendix.
    Organs
    Lymphatic organs include lymph nodes, spleen and thymus. Lymph nodes filter lymph for pathogens, while the spleen filters blood for pathogens, and the thymus is the location of lymphocyte maturation (specifically T lymphocytes).

    Specialties

    Allergist
    Diagnoses and treats allergies, asthma and immunologic conditions
    Infectious Disease Specialist
    Physicians focused on the diagnosis and treatment of acute and chronic diseases caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites, and even prions.
    Immunologist
    Research scientists or practicing specialists who study, analyze and/or treat disease processes that involve the immune system.
    Oncologist
    Special training in diagnosing and treating cancer

    Pathologies

    Lymphangioma
    Benign tumor formed by abnormal collection of lymphatic vessels
    Lymphedema
    Localized swelling of the body caused by an abnormal accumulation of lymph.
    Anaphylaxis
    Severe response to an allergen
    Autoimmune disorder
    Large group of diseases in which the immune system produces antibodies against its own tissues
    Immunotherapy
    Treatment which involves stimulating or suppressing the immune response
    Immunosuppressant
    Substance that prevents or reduces the body’s normal immune response
    Malaria
    Pathology caused by a parasite that lives in mosquitoes resulting in fever, aches and fatigue
    Herpes zoster
    Known as shingles, a viral infection characterized by painful skin eruptions due to reactivation of varicella (chickenpox)
    Benign
    Non-life threatening; a benign tumor is not a form of cancer but can damage as it grows and places pressure on adjacent tissues
    Carcinoma
    Malignant tumor that occurs in epithelial tissues
    Sarcoma
    Malignant tumor that arises from connective tissues
    Lymphoma
    Malignancies of lymphoid tissues
    COVID-19
    Highly contagious viral infection with wide ranging symptoms such as fever, aches, pneumonia, hypoxia, and loss of taste/smell
