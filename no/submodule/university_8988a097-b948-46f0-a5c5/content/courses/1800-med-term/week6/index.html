    Urinary System

    Functions of the Urinary System

    Kidney nephr/o, ren/o
    Filters blood to remove wastes, maintain electrolyte, and water balance.
    Renal Pelvis pyel/o
    Collects urine produced by the kidneys.
    Urine ur/o, urin/o
    Liquid waste products to be excreted.
    Ureters ureter/o
    Transport urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
    Urinary Bladder cyst/o
    Stores urine until it is excreted.
    Urethra urethr/o
    Transports urine from the bladder through the urethral meatus, where it is excreted.
    Prostate prostat/o
    A gland of the male reproductive system that surrounds the male urethra. Disorders of this gland can disrupt the flow of urine.

    Medical Specialties of the Urinary System

    Nephrologist
    A physician who diagnoses and treats diseases and disorders of the kidneys.
    Urologist
    A physician who diagnoses and treats diseases and disorders of the urinary system of females and the genitourinary system of males.

    Pathology of the Urinary System

    Nephropathy
    Any disease of the kidney.
    Includes both degenerative and inflammatory conditions.
    Renal Failure
    Inability of one or both of the kidneys to perform their functions.
    The body cannot replace damaged nephrons thus when too many nephrons have been destroyed, the result is kidney failure.
    Uremia
    Results from renal failure.
    Kidney function is compromised; Urea and waste products normally secreted in the urine are retained in the blood.
    Acute Renal Failure
    Sudden onset, characterized by uremia.
    Chronic Kidney Disease
    Progressive loss of renal function over months or years.
    Buildup of waste in the blood can be a contributing factor in heart attacks and stroke.
    End-Stage Renal Disease
    Also known as kidney failure and is fatal without dialysis or kidney transplant.
    Nephrotic Syndrome (Nephrosis)
    Group of conditions characterized by excessive amounts of protein lost through the urine, usually caused by damage to the glomeruli.
    Edema
    Excessive fluid accumulation in body tissues.
    Hyperproteinuria
    Abnormally high concentrations of protein in urine.
    Hypoproteinemia
    Abnormally low concentrations of protein in the blood.
    Polycystic kidney disease (PKD)
    Genetic disorder in which numerous fluid-filled cysts occur in the kidneys.
    Nephritis
    Inflammation of the kidney or kidneys.
    Glomerulonephritis
    Inflammation of the glomeruli.
    Nephrolithiasis
    Presence of stones (calculi) in the kidney; Form when waste products in urine separate and crystallize (note stones can occur elsewhere and would be named accordingly).
    Ureterorrhagia
    Discharge of blood from the ureter.
    Cystalgia and Systodynia
    Pain in the bladder.
    Interstitial Cystitis
    Chronic inflammation within the walls of the bladder.
    Vesicovaginal Fistula
    Abnormal opening between the bladder and vagina which allows constant involuntary flow of urine from the bladder into the vagina.
    Neurogenic Bladder
    Interference with the normal nerve pathways associated with urination which may result in incontinence and may prevent the bladder from emptying at all or from emptying completely.
    Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)
    Enlargement of the prostate gland.
    Urinary Tract Infections
    Commonly caused by bacteria and occur more frequently in women.
    Diuresis
    Increased output of urine.
    Dysuria
    Difficult or painful urination.
    Polyuria
    Excessive urination.
    Incontinence
    Inability to control the excretion of urine, feces, or both.

    Nervous System

    Parts of the Brain

    Cerebrum
    The outer portion of the brain which performs higher mental functions, plans and initiates movements and interprets sensory stimuli.
    Diencephalon
    Processes and relays information, regulates biological rhythms and maintains homeostasis. Includes the thalamus, hypothalamus and epithalamus.
    Brainstem
    Maintains homeostasis, reflexes and monitors movement.
    Contains the midbrain, pons and medulla oblongata and is continuous with the brainstem.
    Cerebellum
    Monitors and coordinates movements.

    Medical Specialties Related to the Nervous System and Mental Health

    Anesthesiologist
    Physician specializing in administering anesthesia.
    Anesthetist
    Non-physician specializing in administering anesthesia.
    Neurologist
    Physician specializing in the nervous system.
    Neurosurgeon
    Physician specializing in surgery of the nervous system.
    Psychiatrist
    Physician who specializes in chemical dependencies, emotional problems, and mental illness.
    Psychologist
    Has doctoral degree (PhD or PsyD) and treats emotional problems and mental illness (not a medical doctor).
    Social Worker
    Trained therapist, usually with a master’s degree (MSW).

    Pathology of the Nervous System

    Migraine Headache
    Throbbing pain on one side of the head.
    Sometimes accompanied by nausea, aura, vomiting, and sensitivity to light or sound.
    Hydrocephalus
    Excess cerebrospinal fluid accumulates in the ventricles of the brain.
    Meningitis (Infectious Meningitis)
    Inflammation of meninges of the brain and spinal cord.
    Can be caused by a bacterial or viral infection.
    Encephalitis
    Inflammation of the brain.
    Reye’s Syndrome (RS)
    Disorder in children.
    Sometimes follows a viral illness in which the child was treated with aspirin.
    Tetanus (lockjaw)
    Central nervous system infection caused by a toxin produced by the tetanus bacteria.
    Tourette Syndrome (TS)
    Neurological disorder.
    Involuntary tics, grunts, and utterances.
    Dementia
    Slowly progressive decline in mental abilities.
    Vascular Dementia
    Form of dementia.
    Caused by a stroke or other restriction of blood flow to the brain.
    Alzheimer’s Disease (AD)
    Group of disorders.
    Leading form of dementia.
    A degenerative nerve disease.
    Parkinson’s Disease (PD)
    Chronic, degenerative central nervous disorder.
    Amnesia
    Total or partial inability to recall past experience.
    Cerebral Contusion
    Bruising of brain tissue from bouncing against the skull.
    Cranial Hematoma
    Collection of blood trapped in the tissues of the brain.
    Insomnia
    Prolonged or abnormal inability to sleep.
    Narcolepsy
    Sudden and uncontrollable brief episodes of falling asleep during the day.
    Polio (Poliomyelitis)
    Viral infection of the brainstem and spinal cord.
    Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
    Autoimmune disorder.
    Inflammation causes demyelination.
    Epilepsy
    Chronic neurological condition with recurrent episodes of seizures of varying severity.
    Neuropathy
    Any disease or damage to a nerve.

    Mental Health Disorders

    Anxiety Disorders
    Excessive, irrational dread.
    Fear that is out of proportion to real danger which include panic attacks, panic disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
    Phobias
    Persistent, irrational fear of a specific thing or situation.
    Such as acrophobia (fear of heights), agoraphobia (fear of certain environments), claustrophobia (fear of small or enclosed spaces).
    Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
    Neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by difficulty with social relationships/communication skills.
    Attention Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
    Neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by short attention span/impulsive behavior.
    Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)
    Formerly multiple personality disorder.
    Presence of two or more distinct personalities, which appear to exist within the same individual.
    Bipolar Disorder
    Cycles of severe mood changes shifting between highs and lows.
    Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)
    Depression associated with the decrease in hours of daylight during winter months.
    Schizophrenia
    Psychotic disorder usually characterized by withdrawal from reality.
    Bulimia Nervosa
    Frequent episodes of binge eating followed by self-induced vomiting; excessive exercising; misuse of laxatives, diuretics, or other medications.

    claustrophobia klaws-troh-FOH-bee-ah

    coma KOH-mah
