    Bones

    Axial Skeleton: bones of skull, thoracic cage and vertebral column Appendicular Skeleton: bones of the upper and lower extremities

    oss/e, oss/I, osteo/o, ost/o refer to bones. arthr/o refers to joints.

    Bone Marrow
    Related Combining Form(s): myel/o (also means spinal cord)
    Primary Function(s):
    • Red bone marrow forms some blood cells.
    • Yellow bone marrow stores fat.
    Cartilage
    Related Combining Form(s): chondr/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Creates a smooth surface for motion within the joints.
    • Protects the ends of the bones.
    Joints
    Related Combining Form(s): arthr/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Work with the muscles to make a variety of motions possible.
    Ligaments
    Related Combining Form(s): ligament/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Connect one bone to another.
    Synovial Membrane
    Related Combining Form(s): synovi/o, synov/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Forms the lining of synovial joints.
    • Secretes synovial fluid, the lubricant that reduces friction between joints.
    Bursa
    Related Combining Form(s): burs/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Cushions areas of joints that are subject to friction during movement.

    Healthcare

    Chiropractor
    Specialists in manipulative treatment of disorders originating from misalignment of the spine.
    Orthopedic Surgeon
    Physician who specializes in diseases and disorders involving bones, joints, and muscles.
    Podiatrist
    Specializes in diagnosing and treating disorders of the foot.
    Rheumatologist
    Specializes in diagnosing and treatment of arthritis as well as osteoporosis, fibromyalgia, and tendinitis that are characterized by inflammation in the joints and surrounding connective tissues.

    Pathologies

    Ankylosis
    Loss or absence of motility in a joint due to disease, injury, or surgical procedure.
    Note: ankyl means crooked, bent, or stiff.
    Arthrosclerosis
    Stiffness of the joints, especially in the elderly.
    Bursitis
    Inflammation of the bursa.
    Dislocation
    Displacement of a bone from its joint.
    Arthritis
    Inflammation of a joint.
    Note: There are over 100 types of arthritis and related diseases (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis).
    Scoliosis
    Abnormal lateral curvature of the spine.
    Osteomalacia
    Abnormal softening of bone.
    Clubfoot
    Congenital deformity of the foot.
    Osteoporosis
    Loss of bone density and an increase in bone porosity, frequently associated with aging.
    Fracture
    Broken bone.
    Note: There are many types of fractures.

    Muscles

    The Muscular System
    Related Combining Form(s): my/o, myos/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Make body movement possible.
    • Hold body erect.
    • Move body fluids.
    • Produce body heat.
    Fascia
    Related Combining Form(s): fasci/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Cover, support, and separate muscles.
    Tendons
    Related Combining Form(s): ten/o, tend/o, tendin/o
    Primary Function(s):
    • Attach muscles to bones.

    Types

    Skeletal muscle
    found attached to bones; responsible for voluntary movement of the body
    Smooth muscle
    located in blood vessels and digestive tract; responsible for involuntary movement of fluid
    Cardiac muscle
    located in the heart; responsible for involuntary contraction of heart which pumps blood

    Pathology

    Achilles tendinitis
    A painful inflammation of the Achilles tendon caused by excessive stress placed on the tendon
    Ataxia
    Lack of muscle coordination during voluntary movement
    Atrophy
    The wasting or thinning of tissue (muscle atrophy refers to muscle tissue)
    Dyskinesia
    Distortion or impairment of voluntary movement resulting in fragmented or jerky motions
    Hernia
    Occurs when a tissue protrudes through a weak area in the muscle normally containing it
    Muscular dystrophy
    General term for a large group of diseases that are characterized by progressive muscular weakness and degeneration of the skeleton muscles that control movement
    Paraplegia
    Paralysis of both legs and lower part of the body
    Plantar fasciitis
    Inflammation of the plantar fascia on the sole of the foot
    Sarcopenia
    Loss of muscle mass, strength and the function that come with aging
    Torticollis
    Stiff neck due to spasmodic contraction of the neck muscles that pull the head to the affected side
